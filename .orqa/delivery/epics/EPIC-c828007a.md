## Context

The artifact graph is the foundation for everything OrqaStudio does — workflow guards, knowledge injection, traceability pipelines, and state machine transitions all depend on graph queries. Three foundational issues must be resolved before building on top:

1. **Stored inverses create bloat** — every forward relationship requires a stored inverse on the target, causing persona/pillar/milestone files to grow unboundedly
2. **From/to constraints are too narrow** — some relationship types can't be used where they semantically belong (e.g. `informs` restricted to research-only source)
3. **Vocabulary is undocumented** — 30 relationship types across 2 plugins with no knowledge artifacts explaining the semantic distinctions

Additionally: artifact IDs need migrating to deterministic title hashes, the artifact state machine needs reviewing against the plugin-owned model from RES-55bacef1, and `tmp/` needs renaming to `.state/` per AD-8727f99a.

## Risk

Drift during this phase is the major risk. The team design research (RES-55bacef1) recommends specific patterns for orchestrating teams and creating agents. We should follow those recommendations as closely as possible during implementation — even before the formal infrastructure is in place. This means: ephemeral task-scoped agents, hub-spoke orchestration, findings-to-disk, and role-based tool constraints.
